Imam Bayildi

Description

Imam Bayildi is a traditional Turkish dish made from eggplant stuffed with vegetables and topped with olive oil. The stuffing is mostly made from tomatoes, garlic, parsley, onions and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 pcs large aubergine
  • 1 pcs onion peeled roughly chopped
  • 2 pcs tomatoes peeled deseeded and chopped
  • 4 pcs Garlic cloves chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh parsley chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh mint chopped
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 350 ml Olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • A pinch ground cinnamon
  • A pinch Black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Salt

Steps

  1. Peel the aubergines with a vegetable peeler so you get a zebra look like on the image. Halve them lengthwise into two parts. You can use four smaller instead of two large aubergines, but in that case, you prepare them whole. I prefer halved ones because they give me a better visual appearance.
  2. Sprinkle the aubergines with salt and allow 15 minutes for the salt to draw moisture from them. Be sure to wipe them off with a paper towel. Otherwise, they will be soggy.
  3. In a frying pan, heat the olive oil and fry the aubergines on both sides. Fry for 4-5 minutes until they start to get brown colour. When done, remove them from the oil and set aside.
  4. In the second frying pan, we will now prepare the stuffing. Heat a little olive oil and sauté onion, garlic and tomato. Season with salt and pepper and finally add the spices - sugar, cinnamon, parsley and mint.
  5. Make a notch through the centre of the aubergine and fill it with stuffing.
  6. Lay the stuffed aubergines in a baking tray. If there is stuffing leftover, put it in the baking tray next to them. Pour over some more olive oil and lemon juice.
  7. Cover and place in the oven to bake for approx. 40-45 min at 160 degrees.
  8. Remove from oven and allow to cool and then cover with the liquid left from baking.
  9. Imam Bayildi is usually served cold and is additionally garnished with chopped fresh parsley. Yoghurt also goes excellent with this dish.
